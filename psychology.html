Question 1 of 3
Psychology · Social InfluenceConcept

A student confuses normative social influence (NSI) and informational social influence (ISI). Which correctly distinguishes them?

NSI = "I'll go along so they like me." ISI = "They probably know better than me." Keep the driver in mind, not just the letters. ✦

Psychology · AttachmentEvaluation

Which of the following is a valid evaluation of the Strange Situation?

Standardised and replicable, but artificial (lab-based) and culturally biased — German infants are often classified as avoidant, reflecting cultural norms rather than insecurity. ✦

Psychology · PsychopathologyConcept

Statistical infrequency as a definition of abnormality classifies behaviour as abnormal when:

2+ standard deviations from the mean. Objective and quantifiable — but some rare traits (genius IQ) are desirable, which is exactly why the definition has limits. ✦
