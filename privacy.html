Privacy & Data

How Forge collects, uses and protects personal data — for students, teachers, and the schools that purchase Forge. This document also serves as our Data Processing Agreement for institutional customers.

Plain-English summary — Forge collects only what it needs to work. Students using the free tier enter only a name. Students who create a Pro or trial account provide an email address and password — these are stored securely via Supabase Auth and never shared with third parties for commercial purposes. We do not sell data, run ads, or track you across the web. Teachers using school licences do not need personal accounts at the free tier.

1. Who we are

Forge is operated by Forge Education ("we", "us", "our"). For the purposes of UK data protection law, we are the data controller for personal data collected directly through the Forge platform (forgelearning.github.io and any successor domain).

Where Forge is purchased by a school or multi-academy trust, the school is the data controller for student and staff data processed on its behalf, and Forge acts as a data processor. The terms governing that relationship are set out in Section 8 (Data Processing Agreement).

Our contact details are set out in Section 10.

2. What personal data we collect

Free-tier students (no account)

Students using the free tier do not create an account. They enter only:

  • A display name (first name or chosen alias — we do not require a surname)

A random anonymous identifier is generated and stored in their browser (localStorage) and in our database to persist progress on that device. No email address, password, or personal contact information is collected.

Students joining with a class code

Students joining a teacher's class enter a display name and their teacher's class code. The same data as the free tier is collected, plus the class association. No email address is required.

4. Your rights under UK GDPR

If you are based in the UK or EEA, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restriction — ask us to limit how we process your data
  • Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the address in Section 10. We will respond within one calendar month.

Where a student's data is processed under a school DPA, rights requests relating to that student should be directed to the school in the first instance, as the data controller. The school may then request action from us as processor.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or by phone on 0303 123 1113.

6. Security

All data is stored in Supabase (see Section 9), which provides:

  • Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+)
  • Row-level security policies — students can only read their own responses
  • SOC 2 Type II certification
  • Data hosted in EU-West (Ireland) by default

Student Pro/trial accounts are authenticated using Supabase Auth. Passwords are hashed with bcrypt before storage — neither Forge nor Supabase can recover a plaintext password. Access tokens are short-lived (1 hour) and refreshed automatically using a long-lived refresh token stored in browser localStorage.

Access to the teacher dashboard is controlled by class codes. We recommend teachers treat class codes as they would any access credential — do not share them publicly.

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in risk to individuals, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours and affected schools within the same window, as required by Article 33 UK GDPR.

7. Cookies and local storage

Forge does not use cookies. We use browser localStorage to:

  • Remember your theme preference (dark/light mode)
  • Store your session (display name, class code) so you do not have to re-enter it when navigating between the Quiz, Anvil, Crucible and Profile pages
  • Store your anonymous free-tier identifier (a randomly generated token, not linked to any personal information)
  • Store your Supabase Auth session token (access token + refresh token) if you have a Pro or trial account — this allows you to stay logged in across visits without re-entering your password

localStorage data is stored on your device only and is never transmitted to our servers except as part of normal authenticated requests. It can be cleared at any time by clicking "Switch student" / "Sign out" or clearing your browser data.

We do not use analytics cookies, advertising cookies, or any third-party tracking scripts.

8. Data Processing Agreement for schools

Data Processing Agreement

This agreement is entered into between the school or institution purchasing a Forge licence ("Controller") and Forge Education ("Processor"). It supplements and forms part of the Forge licence agreement. Where no separate licence agreement exists, acceptance of these terms via the waitlist or pilot sign-up constitutes agreement.

8.1 Subject matter and duration

The Processor shall process personal data on behalf of the Controller for the purpose of providing the Forge educational platform, including the Quiz, Anvil, Crucible, and Teacher Dashboard. Processing shall continue for the duration of the licence and for the retention periods set out in Section 5.

8.2 Nature of processing

Processing includes: collection, storage, retrieval, consultation, use, and disclosure by transmission of student question responses, misconception tags, display names, and XP data, as described in Section 2.

8.3 Categories of data subjects

Students enrolled in classes created by the Controller's teachers. Teaching and administrative staff of the Controller who access the Teacher Dashboard.

8.4 Types of personal data

Display names (not necessarily legal names), question response data, misconception tags, XP and streak data, session timestamps. See Section 2 for the full list.

8.5 Processor obligations

The Processor shall:

  • Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller (which includes this agreement and any configuration made through the platform)
  • Ensure that persons authorised to process the data are subject to confidentiality obligations
  • Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures as described in Section 6
  • Respect the conditions for engaging sub-processors as set out in Section 9
  • Assist the Controller in responding to requests from data subjects exercising their rights under UK GDPR
  • Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with its obligations regarding security, breach notification, data protection impact assessments, and prior consultation
  • Delete or return all personal data upon termination of the licence, at the Controller's choice, and delete existing copies unless storage is required by applicable law
  • Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this agreement

8.6 Sub-processing

The Controller grants general authorisation for the Processor to engage the sub-processors listed in Section 9. The Processor shall inform the Controller of any intended changes to sub-processors, giving the Controller the opportunity to object. Where the Controller objects and the parties cannot resolve the objection, the Controller may terminate the licence.

8.7 International transfers

All personal data is stored in Supabase's EU-West (Ireland) region. No personal data is transferred outside the UK or EEA in the ordinary course of processing. Should any transfer be necessary, the Processor shall ensure an appropriate safeguard is in place (UK adequacy decision, SCCs, or equivalent).

8.8 Controller obligations

The Controller shall:

  • Ensure it has a lawful basis for processing student data and has fulfilled any transparency obligations to students and parents under UK GDPR (e.g. by including Forge in its school privacy notice)
  • Ensure that class codes are distributed only to enrolled students
  • Notify the Processor promptly if it becomes aware of any breach involving Forge data
  • Not instruct the Processor to process data in a way that would violate applicable data protection law

8.9 Liability and indemnity

Each party shall be responsible for compliance with its own obligations under UK GDPR. The Processor's liability to the Controller under this agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Controller in the twelve months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

8.10 Governing law

This agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

10. Deleting your Pro or trial account

If you have a Pro or trial account and wish to delete it:

  • Email us at forge.educator@gmail.com from the email address associated with your account, with the subject line "Delete my account"
  • We will delete your account and all associated personal data (email, password hash, subjects, responses) within 30 days and confirm by email
  • A pseudonymised transaction record (no email, no name) will be retained for 7 years if you held a paid subscription, for accounting compliance

Free-tier students (no account) can delete their local data by clearing their browser's localStorage or by clicking "Switch student" in the quiz. Their server-side anonymous record will be automatically purged after 12 months of inactivity.

If you have also linked your account to a teacher's class, deletion of your account will remove you from that teacher's dashboard.
