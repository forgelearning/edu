What Forge is

Forge is a web-based A Level revision platform that gives students multiple-choice questions mapped to their exam board's specification, identifies the specific misconceptions behind every wrong answer, and surfaces those patterns to both the student and their teacher.

No. Forge does not use AI to generate questions, explanations, or feedback. Every question, every distractor, and every misconception tag has been written and reviewed by subject specialists against published examiners' reports.

The "intelligence" in Forge is in the question bank and the misconception taxonomy — not a language model. This matters because AI-generated questions can contain factual errors, reward the wrong reasoning, or drift away from what an examiner actually wants. Forge's content is fixed, auditable, and exam-board specific.

Most revision apps test whether a student knows the right answer. Forge is specifically designed around why students get things wrong. Every wrong-answer option is linked to a documented misconception drawn from examiners' reports — not just a plausible distractor.

When a student selects a wrong answer, Forge:

  • Identifies the specific misconception triggered
  • Shows a targeted scaffold explaining exactly what went wrong
  • Queues a re-forge question to test whether the misconception has been corrected
  • Logs the pattern so the teacher can see it in their dashboard

Seneca and Quizlet do not surface misconception data to teachers. Forge does.

Forge currently has question banks for 18 A Level subjects across Edexcel, AQA, OCR, WJEC, and Eduqas specifications:

  • History (AQA 7042) · Law (AQA 7162) · Criminology (WJEC) · Politics (Edexcel 9PL0)

What Forge does

The teacher dashboard gives class-level visibility without requiring teachers to mark or monitor individually:

  • Misconception heatmap — which spec points are firing most frequently across the class
  • Class overview — every student's accuracy, weakest topic, and top misconception, sorted by who is struggling most
  • Student drill-down — click any student to see their full misconception profile and re-forge history
  • Corrective starter generator — Forge proposes a warm-up activity based on the class's top three misconceptions, ready to drop into a lesson
  • Assignments — teachers can set specific banks as homework with a due date; the dashboard shows who has and hasn't started
  • School Overview — a separate read-only view for SLT and Heads of Department showing cross-class trends, engagement rates, and a school-wide misconception league table

Forge is designed to augment teaching, not replace it. It works best as:

  • A structured homework or directed study task between lessons
  • A pre-lesson warm-up (the teacher sets a bank; students spend 5–10 minutes before the lesson starts)
  • Spaced retrieval practice in the weeks before exams
  • A diagnostic tool — teachers can see which misconceptions are live before planning a lesson

Forge does not deliver explanatory content, mark extended answers, or teach new material. The teacher remains the expert; Forge provides the data.

A typical workflow:

  1. Teacher sets a Forge bank as homework before a lesson (e.g. the Inflation bank before a lesson on monetary policy)
  2. Students complete questions at home — Forge logs every response
  3. Teacher opens the dashboard before the lesson and sees the class's top misconceptions
  4. Teacher uses the corrective starter to address the top-firing errors at the start of the lesson
  5. Students who are still firing misconceptions are flagged; teacher knows who to target

This loop closes the gap between what students think they know and what examiners reward.

Inclusion & access arrangements
Ofsted context: Forge was designed with inclusion as a core principle, not an afterthought. The sections below explain how Forge supports students with a range of needs and what is still being built.

Teachers can set a per-student extra time arrangement directly in the class overview. The options are Standard, +25% extra time, and +50% extra time. This setting affects The Crucible (the timed exam-pressure mode) — a student with +25% gets 375 seconds instead of 300.

The arrangement is:

  • Set by the teacher in the dashboard — the student does not need to self-declare or opt in
  • Visible to the student on The Crucible ready screen ("your time arrangement has been applied")
  • Stored against the student's record and applied automatically on every subsequent session

The main quiz (Forge mode) is untimed — there is no clock and no penalty for taking longer. Only The Crucible has a timer, and it is an optional mode.

Forge is built in plain HTML with no canvas elements, so it is fully compatible with screen readers and browser-based accessibility tools. Students can:

  • Increase text size using browser zoom without breaking the layout
  • Use the browser's built-in reader mode on supported browsers
  • Switch to light mode for higher contrast if preferred
  • Use browser extensions such as Texthelp Read&Write, since the page is standard HTML
In development: A dedicated high-contrast mode and a dyslexia-friendly font toggle (OpenDyslexic) are on the near-term roadmap. Formal WCAG 2.1 AA audit is planned before wider rollout.

Several features are specifically designed to prevent low-attaining students from feeling penalised:

  • Scaffolds on every wrong answer — no student sees just "incorrect". Every wrong answer triggers a targeted explanation of what went wrong and why the right answer is right
  • Re-forge attempts — after a wrong answer, students get a second chance on a related question testing the same concept. This means every mistake is an opportunity rather than a dead end
  • Spaced repetition — questions the student has previously struggled with are weighted to appear more often, without the student knowing. The system works hardest on their weakest areas
  • No leaderboard pressure — the class overview is for the teacher only. Students see their own profile but not a ranked list of classmates

Currently, all question content is in English and there is no translation functionality. Students with EAL can use browser-based translation tools (such as Google Translate's page translation) as Forge is standard HTML — it does not block or disable translation extensions.

Planned: Glossary tooltips for subject-specific technical vocabulary are planned for the next development phase. These would give quick definitions of terms like "price elasticity" or "mens rea" on hover, reducing the vocabulary barrier without changing the question content.

Yes. Forge is a web application that runs in any modern browser — Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge — on any device including Chromebooks, iPads, Android tablets, phones, and laptops. There is nothing to install.

Students with a Pro or trial account can log in from any device with their email and password. Their data — responses, misconception history, and progress — is stored in the cloud and syncs across all devices automatically.

This is particularly relevant for students who do not have a personal device at home, as they can use a school Chromebook or library computer without losing their progress.

Forge requires an internet connection and does not currently offer an offline mode. For students without home internet access, we recommend:

  • Setting Forge sessions during school study periods or after-school provision where devices and wifi are available
  • Using school library or computer room time
Planned: A lightweight offline mode — allowing a pre-loaded bank to be completed without internet and synced when connectivity is restored — is under consideration for a future release.
Data protection & privacy
Summary: Forge collects only what it needs to function. It does not sell data, run advertising, or share student information with third parties for commercial purposes. Full details are in the Privacy Policy.

Forge is designed to comply with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. The key points:

  • Data controller: Forge (operated by Mr M Zanier, a practising teacher)
  • Legal basis: Contract (for Pro/trial accounts where a student has signed up) and Legitimate Interests (for class-joined students, where the school has a legitimate educational interest in tracking progress)
  • What is collected: Display name, question responses, misconception tags, and (for Pro/trial accounts) email address and hashed password. No home addresses, phone numbers, photographs, or government identifiers are collected
  • Data processors: Supabase (database and authentication, EU-West/Ireland, SOC 2 Type II) and Stripe (payment processing, PCI DSS Level 1)
  • Retention: Student response data is retained for the academic year plus one additional year, then deleted. Account data is deleted within 30 days of a deletion request

A full Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available for schools that require one. Contact forge.educator@gmail.com.

It depends on how they access Forge:

  • Class-joined students — enter a display name (first name + surname initial, e.g. "James B") and their teacher's class code. No email address is required
  • Free-tier students — enter only a display name. No email, no account
  • Pro/trial subscribers — provide an email address and create a password. This is necessary to enable cross-device login and is stored securely via Supabase Auth (bcrypt hashed; neither Forge nor Supabase can recover a plaintext password)

For most school use cases — where students join via a class code — no email address is ever collected from students.

Student data is stored in Supabase's EU-West (Ireland) data centre. Ireland is an adequacy country under UK GDPR, so no additional transfer mechanisms are required.

Stripe (used for payment processing only — not for storing student response data) processes globally but operates under Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for international transfers.

Forge does not use US-based databases, analytics services, or advertising networks for student data.

For schools on a paid licence, a formal DPA is available and recommended. It covers the processing activities, sub-processors, data subject rights, breach notification procedures, and retention schedules.

For individual teachers using Forge informally (e.g. as a free trial), a DPA is not legally required if students join via class code only (no email collection). However, we recommend notifying students and parents that their display name and response data are stored in accordance with the privacy policy.

Contact forge.educator@gmail.com to request a DPA.

Yes. Under UK GDPR, students (or their parents/carers if under 16) have the right to request erasure of their personal data. To exercise this right:
